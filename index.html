Biomechanics of Movement
Uchida · Delp · Delp
The Science of Sports, Robotics & Rehabilitation

How does the body move, and how do we move better?

From the inverted pendulum of a walking stride to the muscle-driven simulations that power modern robotics and rehabilitation — this book examines human and animal movement through the lens of mechanics.

01 Overview

An engaging introduction to human and animal movement, seen through the lens of mechanics.

How do Olympic sprinters run so fast? Why do astronauts adopt a bounding gait on the moon? How do running shoes improve performance while preventing injuries? This generously illustrated book answers these questions by examining movement through simple conceptual models — from walking and running to the microscopic machinery of a single muscle fiber.

Drawing on established principles and recent innovations in computer simulation, mobile motion monitoring, and wearable robotics, the book is built for students, clinicians, roboticists, engineers, sports scientists, and anyone curious about the mechanics of how we move.
